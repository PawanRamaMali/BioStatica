Introduction

BioStatica delivers a structured learning pathway—from statistical fundamentals to advanced methods in biostatistics—blending clarity with rigor for learners at all levels. Whether you're a beginner starting your journey in statistics or an advanced practitioner looking to enhance your skills, our comprehensive curriculum will guide you through every step.

1. Foundational Concepts

Descriptive Statistics & Visualization

Explore summaries like mean, variance, and the use of histograms and boxplots to understand your data distribution and characteristics.

Probability & Distributions

Cover discrete and continuous distributions, normal and t-distributions, and the fundamental principles of probability theory.

Sampling & Sampling Distributions

Understand central limit theorem and the behavior of means, essential for making inferences about populations.

Point & Interval Estimation

Learn about confidence intervals and estimation accuracy to quantify uncertainty in your statistical estimates.

Hypothesis Testing Framework

Learn null vs alternative hypotheses, Type I and II errors, p-values, and the logical foundation of statistical testing.

One-Sample & Two-Sample t-Tests

Introduce t-test basics under equal-variance assumptions as core foundational tools for comparing means.

2. Comparative Inference

Welch's t-Test & Paired t-Test

Robust comparisons for unequal variances or paired samples, essential when assumptions of equal variance are violated.

Non-Parametric Alternatives

Covers Mann–Whitney, Kruskal–Wallis, and Wilcoxon tests for situations where normal distribution assumptions don't hold.

3. Multi-Group Analysis

ANOVA & Post-hoc Comparisons

Methods like ANOVA, Tukey's HSD, and multiple comparisons controls for analyzing differences among three or more groups.

4. Interrelationships & Modeling

Correlation & Simple Linear Regression

Measure relationships between variables and understand the proportion of variance explained in simple regression models.

Multiple Regression & Logistic Regression

Modeling multiple predictors and binary outcomes, essential for real-world data analysis with complex relationships.

5. Design & Power Considerations

Sample Size & Statistical Power

Planning for sufficient power and error control to ensure your studies can detect meaningful effects when they exist.

Multiple Testing Corrections

Adjustments like Bonferroni and FDR (False Discovery Rate) for controlling error rates when testing multiple hypotheses.

6. Specialized & Modern Methods

Survival Analysis

Kaplan–Meier curves and Cox proportional hazards models for time-to-event data analysis in medical research.

Causal Inference

Techniques for estimating treatment effects from observational data, crucial for understanding cause-and-effect relationships.

Missing Data & Imputation

Strategies for managing incomplete data, including multiple imputation and sensitivity analysis techniques.

Meta-Analysis

Combining study results using fixed and random effects models to synthesize evidence across multiple studies.

Bayesian Methods

Incorporating prior knowledge into inference, offering a flexible framework for statistical modeling and uncertainty quantification.

Machine Learning & High-Throughput Data

Analysis of genomics and big data methods, bridging traditional statistics with modern computational approaches.

Interactive Tools

Enhance your learning with hands-on, interactive resources designed to reinforce key concepts and provide practical experience.

R Notebooks

Interactive R notebooks with real datasets and guided exercises

Python Modules

Python-based statistical analysis with popular libraries like pandas and scipy

Quizzes

Self-assessment quizzes to test your understanding of key concepts

Dynamic Visualizations

Interactive charts and graphs to explore statistical concepts visually
